# Yamacraw Business Portal — Demo Video Narration Script

**Total runtime target: ~2:35**
**10 lines, recorded one-by-one, each within its target duration.**

---

## How this works

Record each line **as its own audio file** (one take per line, clean cuts at head and tail). The editor will drop each take into its matching slot in the video. Hit the target duration within ±1 second — if you go longer, it cuts into the next visual; shorter is fine, silence gets padded automatically.

**Recording setup:**
- Quiet room, no fan or AC hum.
- Mic 6–8 inches from your mouth, slightly off-axis.
- MP3 or WAV export at 44.1 kHz, mono or stereo — both fine.
- Filename each take as shown (`01_hero.mp3`, `02_featured.mp3`, etc.) so the editor can drop them straight in.
- Leave ~0.5 seconds of clean silence at the head and tail of every clip.

**Tone overall:** Warm, confident, civic. This is a community directory sponsored by a minister's office — think NPR host meets community radio. Friendly but composed. Not corporate, not political.

**Pace:** Natural conversational speed. The targets below were timed from a neutral-pace AI voice, so you've got room — just don't rush.

**Pronunciations to note:**
- **Yamacraw** — *YAM-uh-craw* (emphasis on first syllable)
- **Zane Lightbourne** — *ZAYNE LITE-born*
- **Kalik / conch / junkanoo** — not in the script, but if needed: *kah-LEEK, konk, junk-uh-NOO*

## Line 1 — "Welcome" (Hero)

**Target: 13 seconds · On-screen: animated homepage hero with the portal title**
**Tone: warm open; inviting; like you're welcoming someone into a room**

> Welcome to the Yamacraw Business Portal — a civic directory built for the Yamacraw community in The Bahamas. We connect residents with the local businesses, contractors, and service providers that make their neighbourhood thrive.

---

## Line 2 — "Featured Listings"

**Target: 14 seconds · On-screen: featured carousel scrolling through demo businesses**
**Tone: informative, a touch of pride; "look what we've got"**

> Right from the homepage, you can see featured listings — from family-run restaurants to government-contracted tradesmen. Each listing is reviewed and approved before it goes live, so residents know what they're looking at is trusted.

---

## Line 3 — "Browse by Category"

**Target: 13 seconds · On-screen: clicking through category filter pills on the directory page**
**Tone: helpful, practical; slightly lighter energy**

> Browsing by category is the fastest way to find what you need. Restaurants, beauty and wellness, auto services, construction trades — every type of business that serves the Yamacraw area, one click away.

*(The category list is a small pacing pocket — don't race through it, but don't drag either. Think of it like ticking off a to-do list.)*

---

## Line 4 — "The Full Directory"

**Target: 14 seconds · On-screen: contractor tab + directory scroll**
**Tone: confident, practical; slightly faster than line 2**

> The full directory lets you filter by category, or jump to contractors specifically — handy for government tenders or major home projects. Every card shows a logo, a rating, and a quick summary, so you can scan fast.

---

## Line 5 — "Business Profile"

**Target: 15 seconds · On-screen: scrolling through a full business detail page**
**Tone: reassuring; the punchline is "they don't have to dig through social media"**

> Opening a listing gives you everything you need in one place: photos, reviews, operating hours, contact details, and a direct inquiry form. Residents don't have to dig through social media — they reach the business straight from the portal.

---

## Line 6 — "Why This Exists" ⭐ **(the emotional beat)**

**Target: 23 seconds · On-screen: About page with Minister Lightbourne's photo + mission tiles**
**Tone: slower, warmer, sincere. Not preachy. This is the heart of the video.**
**Pause briefly after "a reason." and again after "find them."**

> But this portal exists for a reason. *(brief pause)* Minister Zane Lightbourne and his office recognised that talented Yamacraw businesses and tradesmen were going unseen — not because they weren't great at what they do, but because residents simply couldn't find them. *(brief pause)* This isn't just a website. It's an investment in the people who make Yamacraw feel like home.

*(End on a warm, resolved note — "…feel like home." should land.)*

---

## Line 7 — "Owner Dashboard"

**Target: 12 seconds · On-screen: logging in and landing on the business owner dashboard**
**Tone: back to informative; you're shifting audiences to business owners**

> For the business owners and contractors in our community, the portal is just as simple. After signing up, you land on a dashboard that shows all your listings, views, inquiries, and reviews at a glance.

---

## Line 8 — "Create a Listing"

**Target: 12 seconds · On-screen: the create-listing form**
**Tone: brisk, practical; you're walking through a how-to**

> Creating a listing takes a few minutes — name, category, description, hours, photos, and you're submitted for admin review. Once approved, you're discoverable to everyone in Yamacraw and beyond.

---

## Line 9 — "Stay Responsive" (+ Contact mention)

**Target: 14 seconds · On-screen: inquiry inbox → detail → Contact page**
**Tone: practical in the first half, gentle invitation in the second**

> Inquiries from residents arrive straight in the business's inbox. They can reply, close, or mark as spam — everything they need to stay responsive. And for residents with questions of their own, the Contact page is always just one click away.

---

## Line 10 — "Outro / Motto" ⭐ **(timed to animations)**

**Target: 5 seconds · On-screen: navy gradient card; title rolls in first, then the motto**
**Tone: deliberate, earned, final. Let the words breathe.**

> The Yamacraw Business Portal. *(half-beat pause — the title animates in here)* Built by the community, owned by the community.

*(This one needs to feel like a mission statement. Don't race it — the animation waits for you.)*

## Delivery checklist

- [ ] Room is quiet (AC off, phone silenced, no computer fan)
- [ ] Same mic distance and angle for every line (keeps volume consistent)
- [ ] Water nearby — stay off coffee / dairy, they muddy the voice
- [ ] Do a test recording, listen back, check for plosives ("p" / "b" pops) and sibilance ("s" hiss)
- [ ] Record each line 2–3 times, pick the best take
- [ ] Export as MP3 (192 kbps+) or WAV, 44.1 kHz
- [ ] Zip the 10 files and send them back — no need to edit or align, that's handled in post
